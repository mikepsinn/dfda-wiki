A New and Improved FDA Mandate

The US Food and Drug Administration was created for the very important purpose of protecting and promoting human health and safety. Unfortunately, the 1962 Congressional efficacy mandate that drives all FDA policies is terribly worded. It's so flawed that it actually prevents the FDA from effectively carrying out its true purpose.

It's currently mandated to ensure the "efficacy of drugs." This is obviously an ideal goal, but the only way to perfectly fulfill this mandate is to ensure that no one ever takes a new drug again. That would 100% guarantee that no one ever has the opportunity to take a drug that may or may not be effective.

In practice, we've seen a less absolutist interpretation of the mandate. Instead of completely rejecting all new treatments, they have instead exponentially increased the regulatory barrier. Since 1962, when the current efficacy mandate was adopted, the cost of bringing a new treatment to market has increased 15X (1521%).
